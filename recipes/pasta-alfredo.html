The most infamous pasta: The Alfredo's

Description

Traditional Alfredo is a simple white/cream sauce. It is made from butter, heavy cream, and parmesan cheese. While the original style is great, I simply like to add garlic, salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning to make Alfredo sauce taste even better!

Not my fault, found this description on the web

Ingredients

  • 500 g fettuccine
  • 80 g butter
  • 80 g Parmesan Cheese
  • salt to taste
  • pepper to taste
    •

    Steps

    1. First, bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil and cook the fettuccine until al dente. (About 6 minutes)

    2. Melt the butter in a large frying pan over low heat. Then add a ladle of pasta water and mix thoroughly to obtain a creamy sauce.

    3. Once the fettuccine are al dente, drain directly into the pan with a pasta tong. Now add another ladle of pasta water and mix everything quickly and well.

    4. Then turn off the heat, add freshly grated parmesan and season with salt and pepper. Mix thoroughly again to combine the sauce with the pasta.

    5. Finally, pour the finished fettuccine Alfredo on deep plates and enjoy immediately.

    Buon appetito!
